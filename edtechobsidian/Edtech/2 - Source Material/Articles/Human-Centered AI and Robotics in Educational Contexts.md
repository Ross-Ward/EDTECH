## üéØ Multidimensional Research Inquiry

### Core Research Question
How can human-centered AI and robotics enhance educational ecosystems by fostering adaptive, collaborative, and ethically grounded learning experiences?

### Nested Research Sub-Questions
1. **Cognitive Dimension**: How do human-robot interactions influence cognitive processes like attention, memory, and metacognition in learners?
2. **Technological Intervention**: What role do adaptive robotic systems play in personalizing educational interventions?
3. **Learning Ecosystem Impact**: How do these technologies reshape the socio-technical dynamics of educational environments?

## üß≠ Integrated Theoretical Frameworks

### Primary Theoretical Foundations
1. **Cognitive Load Theory**
   - Robots as cognitive scaffolds to manage information processing demands in learning.
   - Example: Reducing extraneous load via intuitive physical or verbal interactions.

2. **Sociocultural Learning Theory**
   - Robots as mediators of social learning through collaborative tasks.
   - Emphasis on situated, embodied interactions in educational settings.

3. **Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)**
   - Factors influencing learner and educator acceptance of robotic assistants.
   - Focus on perceived ease of use and trust in autonomous systems.

### Interdisciplinary Theoretical Intersections
- Neuroscientific insights into brain-robot interaction (e.g., error potential signals).
- Computational modeling of adaptive robot behaviors in classrooms.
- Ethical frameworks for AI-mediated learning environments.
