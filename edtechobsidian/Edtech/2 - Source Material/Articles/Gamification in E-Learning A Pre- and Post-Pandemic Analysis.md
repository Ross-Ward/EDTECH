## üéØ Multidimensional Research Inquiry

### Core Research Question

How did the effects and implementation of gamification in e-learning evolve before and during the COVID-19 pandemic, particularly in terms of game elements, learner behavior, and effectiveness factors?

### Nested Research Sub-Questions

1. **Cognitive Dimension**: How do specific game elements (e.g., badges, levels) influence learners' motivation and engagement?
2. **Technological Intervention**: What shifts in gamification strategies emerged due to the rapid transition to e-learning platforms during the pandemic?
3. **Learning Ecosystem Impact**: How do contextual factors (e.g., platform design, user background) mediate the effectiveness of gamified e-learning?

## üß≠ Integrated Theoretical Frameworks

### Primary Theoretical Foundations

1. **Cognitive Load Theory**
    - Gamification elements like badges and feedback modulate cognitive resource allocation by providing structured incentives.
    - Overuse risks cognitive overload, as noted in challenges with augmented reality (AR) tools.
2. **Sociocultural Learning Theory**
    - Leaderboards and challenges foster social interaction and community coalescence, particularly during pandemic isolation.
    - Competition as a dynamic reflects cultural and social learning contexts.
3. **Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)**
    - User adoption of gamified e-learning hinges on perceived ease of use (e.g., intuitive interfaces) and usefulness (e.g., improved engagement).

## üí° Synthesized Key Insights

Gamification in e-learning evolved significantly from pre- to post-COVID-19 contexts. Pre-pandemic, elements like badges and levels focused on engagement and competition, with limited empirical grounding. During the pandemic, a surge in research highlighted storylines, challenges, and leaderboards as tools for social cohesion amid isolation, though effectiveness remained context-dependent. Cognitive benefits (motivation, attention) coexist with risks (distraction, overload), underscoring the need for balanced design and clearer definitions.
