## üî¨ Advanced Methodological Architecture

### Research Methodology Spectrum
- **[[20250327-Quasi-Experimental-Design]]**: Compares WF (n=38), VFA (n=66), and VFV (n=72) groups over a semester.  
- **Data Collection Strategies**:  
  - [[20250327-Feedback-Perception-Surveys]]: Likert-scale ratings of effectiveness (1-5).  
  - [[20250327-Learning-Interaction-Logs]]: Tracks [[20250327-Feedback-Access-Decisions]] via Moodle data logs.  
  - [[20250327-Academic-Performance-Metrics]]: Course scores and GPA.  
  - [[20250327-Engagement-Assessment-Tools]]: 12-item engagement questionnaire ([[20250327-Cognitive-Engagement]], [[20250327-Affective-Engagement]], [[20250327-Behavioral-Engagement]]).  
- **Analytical Techniques**:  
  - [[20250327-ANCOVA-Analysis]]: Controls for GPA and engagement.  
  - [[20250327-Mediation-Moderation-Analysis]]: Tests engagement‚Äôs role ([[20250327-Structural-Equation-Modeling]]).  

### Key Findings
- **Perceptions**: High satisfaction across all formats ([[20250327-Perceived-Feedback-Effectiveness]] ~4/5), no significant differences.  
- **Feedback Review**: No significant variation in [[20250327-Feedback-Access-Decisions]] across formats, contrary to [[20250327-H2a-Hypothesis]].  
- **Achievement**: VFV and VFA groups outperformed WF ([[20250327-Academic-Performance-Metrics]]), no VFV-VFA difference ([[20250327-H2b-Hypothesis]] partially supported).  
- **Engagement Effects**:  
  - Moderates [[20250327-Feedback-Access-Decisions]]: WF benefits high-engagement students; VFV mitigates low engagement ([[20250327-H3b-Hypothesis]]).  
  - Mediates [[20250327-Academic-Performance-Metrics]]: VFA/VFV boosts engagement, enhancing scores ([[20250327-H4a-Hypothesis]]).
