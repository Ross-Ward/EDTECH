# Student Social Interaction In Online Learning

- **ID:** 20231125A
- **Source:** Ilham, M. (2023). *Student Social Interaction In Online Learning.* *Indonesian Journal of Educational Technology*, 2(2), 42-48. E-ISSN 2962-9225
- **Tags:** #SocialInteraction #OnlineLearning #EducationTechnology #StudentEngagement #FamilyDynamics
- **Date:** 2025-03-25

## Summary
This descriptive-qualitative study at Makassar State University examines social interaction in online learning. It finds that reduced direct interaction with educators and peers hampers educational quality, leading to lower comprehension, network issues, and distractions (e.g., online games). Conversely, it strengthens family bonds due to increased parent-child interaction.

## Key Findings
- **Education Impact:** Less interaction reduces understanding, motivation, and learning outcomes.
- **Challenges:** Network disruptions, task overload, and gaming distractions.
- **Family Benefit:** More time at home fosters parent-child understanding and moral development.
- **Benefits:** Flexibility, motivation, and problem-solving potential via online networks.

## Methodology
- **Design:** Qualitative-descriptive with purposive sampling.
- **Data:** Interviews with students and parents at Makassar State University.
- **Approach:** Narrative focus on observable behaviors and experiences.

## Concepts
- **Social Interaction:** Reciprocal relationships driving social and cognitive growth.
- **Online Learning:** Internet-based education with flexible, yet limited, interaction.
