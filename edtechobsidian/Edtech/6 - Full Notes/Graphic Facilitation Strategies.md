## Summary
Graphic facilitation uses visuals to enhance learning and communication. Cavaliero et al. (n.d.) show its value in Higher Education for engagement and clarity.

## Key Points
- **Visuals:** Icons, mind maps aid comprehension (Sibbet, 2008).
- **Engagement:** Boosts interest, problem-solving (Cavaliero et al., n.d.).
- **Delivery:** Supports teaching, feedback via graphics.
- **Origin:** Emerged in 1970s design contexts.

## Concepts
- **Visual Language:** Simplifies complex ideas.
- **Creative Thinking:** Encourages innovation.
